About

I am currently completing a PhD in Mechanical Engineering and Materials Science at Harvard University in the Weitz Group. My thesis, Elasticity, Wetting, and Energy-Dissipation Near Interfaces, focuses on how complex soft materials behave under deformation, flow, and interfacial interactions.

My background includes experimental mechanics, rheology, microscopy, image analysis, and fluid systems, with additional experience in project engineering and teaching. I am interested in engineering and R&D roles where I can apply quantitative modeling, experiments, and data analysis to real materials and design problems.

Published Research

Contact Angle Mapping Using Micro-Droplets

Built an automated system that deposits microdroplets on surfaces to generate wettability maps for heterogeneous materials.

  • Designed an automated measurement platform for microdroplet deposition
  • Mapped local surface wettability with high spatial resolution
  • Relevant applications in coatings, geology, and biology
  • Langmuir (2025)

Compression-Induced Syneresis of Emulsion-Fiber Composites
